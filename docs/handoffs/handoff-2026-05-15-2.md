## Key Decisions and Why

### Compose existing patterns, don't invent new primitives
- **What:** Use the v2.0.18 – v2.0.22 motion primitives (sine paddle, horizontal patrol, deterministic path) and compose them with existing geometry (mazes, switchbacks, frame walls) to fill the 60-room gap. No engine work in this sprint.
- **Why:** Schema already supports composition (`LayoutTemplate.obstacles[]` + `movingObstacles[]` coexist). Inventing new motion primitives is engine work; composing existing ones is pure content. Each new template costs ~3–5 minutes once the grammar is internalized.
- **Alternative:** New motion primitives (rotating barriers, accelerating obstacles, projectiles) — deferred to follow-up engine work, not in scope.

### Forcing geometry is the recurring fix
- **What:** Every medium/hard template that allows route-around at the top or bottom of the canvas must include either frame walls (corridor-compressing) or wide bar-paddles (corridor-spanning) so the motion can't be bypassed.
- **Why:** Three playtest rounds in a row called out the same failure mode: open geometry + motion plays easier than expected because the player routes around the motion's y-range. The fix that consistently worked: pin the player to a band, or make the paddle wider than the bypass.
- **Alternative:** Just amping periods/amplitude alone — didn't work; route-around survived the speed-up.

### Medium-tempo periods are 2400–3500 ms
- **What:** Established `pendulum`/`shutters` (period 2400 ms) as the medium tempo anchor. New medium templates use periods in 2400–3500 ms range; anything ≥ 4000 ms is at risk of playing easy.
- **Why:** v1 medium batch shipped with 4500–6000 ms periods and all played as easy. Tuning pass brought them into 2400–3500 ms and they landed.

### Direct-commit small playtest patches to `main`
- **What:** PR #95's playtest follow-up (s-curve-patrol → easy + maze-gauntlet wide-bar) landed via direct commit (`2b2c89f`) to main rather than as a follow-up PR.
- **Why:** PR #95 merged before the playtest pass completed. Fix was small (two templates, geometry-only). Project precedent: user previously direct-committed the intern-events refactor to main similarly.
- **Alternative:** Open PR #95.5 as a follow-up. Rejected — too much PR ceremony for a 2-template tuning fix.

## Dead Ends

- **v1 medium templates with 4500–6000 ms periods.** All six PR #95 v1 templates played as easy or simple. The slow tempo gave the player enough time to read each motion and walk through unhurried. **Lesson:** medium-tempo anchor is 2400 ms (per pendulum/shutters), not 5000 ms. Periods above 4000 ms read as easy regardless of geometry.
- **Narrow paddles (20–60 px wide) in wide corridors.** First attempt at switchback-paddle and maze-gauntlet used narrow paddles. Player routed around them at x=300 or x=700 — paddle never engaged. **Lesson:** if the corridor is wider than the paddle, the paddle is decorative. Either widen the paddle to span the corridor OR add walls to narrow the corridor.
- **Sentinel orbits in open rooms.** v1 `slow-orbit` had no static walls; player just routed above or below the orbit's y-range. **Lesson:** path-based sentinels need corridor framing to engage the player. Fixed by adding top + bottom frame walls + renaming to *Corralled orbit*.
- **Gauntlet v1: 3 snake walls + 1 weak central paddle.** Gaps too wide (310 px), single paddle amp 60 swept only 60 px of a 310-px gap. **Lesson:** snake-maze geometry alone isn't enough at hard tier — needs sequential motion challenges, one per corridor.
- **Crossfire v1: 3 motions, called "hard, not expert."** User feedback: "not expert unless you added more pickets." **Lesson:** at expert tier, motion density matters more than motion speed. 3 motions in a half-canvas isn't enough.
